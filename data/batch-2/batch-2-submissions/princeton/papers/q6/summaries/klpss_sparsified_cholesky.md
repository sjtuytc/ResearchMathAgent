# Sparsified Cholesky and Multigrid Solvers for Connection Laplacians (Kyng, Lee, Peng, Sachdeva, Spielman, arxiv:1512.01892)

## Definitions
- **Definition 1.1.** A Hermitian block-matrix $M \in (\mathbb{C}^{r \times r})^{n \times n}$ is block diagonally dominant (or bDD) if for all $i \in [n]$, $M_{[i,i]} \succeq I_r \cdot \sum_{j:j \neq i} \| M_{[i,j]} \|$, where $I_r \in \mathbb{C}^{r \times r}$ denotes the identity matrix.
- **Definition 3.2 (Schur Complement Chain).** An $\epsilon$-Schur complement chain ($\epsilon$-SCC) for a matrix $M^{(0)}$ indexed by vertex set $V$ is a sequence of operators and subsets, $((M^{(1)}, Z^{(1)}), \ldots, (M^{(d)}, Z^{(d)}); F_1, \ldots, F_d)$, so that for $C_0 = V$ and $C_{i+1} = C_i \setminus F_{i+1}, |C_d| \le 1000$ and for $1 \le i \le d$, $M^{(i)} \approx_{\epsilon_i} Sc(M^{(i-1)}, F_i)$ and $0 \preceq (Z^{(i)})^{-1} - M^{(i-1)}_{[F_i,F_i]} \preceq \epsilon_i \cdot Sc(M^{(i-1)}, C_i)$.
- **Definition 3.4.** A Hermitian block-matrix $M$ is $\alpha$-bDD if $\forall i, M_{[i,i]} \succeq (1 + \alpha) I_r \cdot \sum_{j:j \neq i} \| M_{[i,j]} \|$.
- **Definition B.4.** A matrix $B \in (\mathbb{C}^{r \times r})^{n \times m}$ is called a unitary edge-vertex transfer matrix, when each block column of $B_{[e]}$ has exactly two nonzero blocks $U_e, V_e \in \mathbb{C}^{r \times r}$ s.t. $U_e U_e^* = V_e V_e^* = w_e I_r$ where $w_e \ge 0$.
- **Definition G.4.** The product demand block-Laplacian of a vector $d \in (\mathbb{C}^{r \times r})^n$, is a bDD matrix $L_{G(d)} \in (\mathbb{C}^{r \times r})^{n \times n}$, defined as $(L_{G(d)})_{[i,j]} = \begin{cases} - d_{[i]} d_{[j]}^* & i \neq j, \\ I_r \cdot \|d_{[i]}\| \sum_{k:k \neq i} \|d_{[k]}\| & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases}$
- **Definition G.5.** Given a vector $d \in (\mathbb{C}^{r \times r})^n$ and an index set $F \subseteq V$, let $C = V \setminus F$. The bipartite product demand block-Laplacian of $(d, F)$ is a bDD matrix $L_{G(d),F} \in (\mathbb{C}^{r \times r})^{n \times n}$, defined as $(L_{G(d,F)})_{[i,j]} = \begin{cases} I_r \cdot \|d_{[i]}\| \sum_{k \in C} \|d_{[k]}\| & i = j, \text{and } i \in F \\ I_r \cdot \|d_{[i]}\| \sum_{k \in F} \|d_{[k]}\| & i = j, \text{and } i \in C \\ 0 & i \neq j, \text{and } (i, j \in F \text{ or } i, j \in C) \\ - d_{[i]} d_{[j]}^* & i \neq j, \text{otherwise.} \end{cases}$
- **Definition G.13.** The product demand graph of a vector $d \in (\Re_{>0})^n$, $G(d)$, is a complete weighted graph on $n$ vertices whose weight between vertices $i$ and $j$ is given by $w_{ij} = d_i d_j$. The Laplacian of $G(d)$, denoted $L_{G(d)}$ is called the product demand Laplacian of $d$.
- **Definition G.14.** The bipartite product demand graph of two vectors $d_A \in (\Re_{>0})^{|A|}, d_B \in (\Re_{>0})^{|B|}$, $G(d_A, d_B)$, is a weighted bipartite graph on vertices $A \cup B$, whose weight between vertices $i \in A$ and $j \in B$ is given by $w_{ij} = (d_A)_i (d_B)_j$. The Laplacian of $G(d_A, d_B)$, denoted $L_{G(d_A, d_B)}$ is called the bipartite product demand Laplacian of $(d_A, d_B)$.

  *Proof:* Employs Lemma 2.4 to handle zero eigenvalues and invokes Lemma I.5 with $k = \log\log\log n$ to simplify the asymptotic bounds.
- **Theorem 1.3 (Sparsified Cholesky).** For every bDD matrix $M$ with $n$ block-rows there exists a diagonal matrix $D$ and an upper triangular matrix $U$ with $O(n)$ nonzero blocks so that $U^T D U \approx_{3/4} M$. Moreover, linear equations in $U$, $U^T$, and $D$ can be solved with linear work in depth $O(\log^2 n)$, and these matrices can be computed in polynomial time.
  *Proof:* Sets $\epsilon_i = 1/8(i+2)^2$ and iteratively applies Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.8 to find 4-diagonally dominant subsets. Accumulates the block counts and approximation errors via a convergent geometric series to guarantee the linear-sized inverse.
