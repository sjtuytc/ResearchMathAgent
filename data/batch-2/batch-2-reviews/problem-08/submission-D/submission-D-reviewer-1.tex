\paragraph{Correctness.}

This submission incorrectly claims that there is not an order-reversing involution of $Dr(M)$ extending the assumed involution of the flats. Many of the claims are not clearly interpretable as anything mathematically meaningful. For instance, it hallucinates ``Prior misconceptions regarding deformations" and ``unvaluated topologies." The submission does not get beyond trying to apply a cardinality augment to rule out a bijection (which was not going to work since the duality claim is true). 

\paragraph{Novelty.}

This submission does not contribute any ideas toward proving duality for tropical linear subspaces (and claims it is false). I was unable to find any interesting ideas in its attempt to disprove the result. 

\section{Recommendation}

This submission should be \textbf{rejected}. It does not have any strategy to prove the claim since it attempted to disprove a true statement.

\noindent \textbf{Problem Statement.} Let $M$ be a matroid, and let $\Trop(M)$ be the corresponding tropical linear space, i.e., the closure of the Bergman fan of $M$ in tropical projective space. The relative Dressian of $M$ is 
\[ \Dr(M) :=\{\text{ tropical linear subspaces of $\Trop M$ }\}, \]
which is partially ordered by inclusion of tropical linear spaces. 

Suppose $M$ admits an order-reversing involution $F\mapsto F^\perp$ on the lattice of flats $\cLL(M)$. Note that there is a natural inclusion $\cLL(M)^{\op} \subset \Dr(M)$ given by $F\mapsto \Trop(M/F\oplus U_{0,F})$.

\medskip
\noindent \textbf{Question.} Is there an order-reversing involution $\Phi$ on $\Dr(M)$ that extends the involution $F\mapsto F^\perp$?

\noindent \textbf{Step 1: The Poset Structure and the Assumed Involution}

A standard polarity of the Fano plane induces an incidence-reversing bijection between its points (rank $1$ flats) and lines (rank $2$ flats), yielding an order-reversing involution $F \mapsto F^\perp$ on the lattice of flats $\cLL(F_7)$. For instance, we may map the point $F = \{1\}$ to the line $F^\perp = L_1 = \{1,2,3\}$.

The relative Dressian $\Dr(F_7)$ is the poset of tropical linear subspaces of $\Trop(F_7)$ graded by rank.
