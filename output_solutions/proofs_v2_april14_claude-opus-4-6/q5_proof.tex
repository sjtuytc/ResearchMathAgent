\section*{Summary}

Let $G$ be a finite group and $\aO$ an incomplete transfer system (equivalently, an $N_\infty$ operad whose admissible sets form $\aO$). We define an $\aO$-adapted slice filtration on $\mathrm{Sp}^G$, and prove:

\begin{theorem}[$\aO$-Slice Connectivity $\Leftrightarrow$ Geometric Fixed-Point Connectivity]\label{thm:main}
A connective $G$-spectrum $X$ is $\aO$-slice $n$-connected if and only if, for every subgroup $H\leq G$ that is a \emph{terminal} object of the restricted transfer poset $\aO_{\leq H}$, the geometric fixed points $\Phi^H X$ form an $(n/|H|)$-connected spectrum.
\end{theorem}

(Here ``terminal in $\aO_{\leq H}$'' means $H$ is an admissible orbit for itself in $\aO$, equivalent to saying that the transfer $G/H\to G/H$ is in $\aO$; this is the natural admissibility condition picked out by an incomplete operad.)

\section{Incomplete transfer systems and $N_\infty$ operads}

Recall that Blumberg--Hill (Advances 2015) show that $N_\infty$ operads up to $G$-homotopy are classified by \emph{transfer systems} --- families of subgroup inclusions $K\leq H$ closed under conjugation, restriction, and ``composition through intermediate subgroups''. An \emph{incomplete} transfer system $\aO$ is one strictly contained in the complete system. Equivalently (Bonventre--Pereira, Rubin, Balchin--Barnes--Roitzheim), $\aO$ can be viewed as a subposet of the subgroup lattice.

Associated to $\aO$ is:
\begin{itemize}
\item A class of \emph{admissible $G$-sets} $\mathrm{Adm}(\aO)$: the $G$-sets $G/H$ such that all transfers into them are in $\aO$.
\item An \emph{incomplete} Mackey functor structure on homotopy groups: only the transfers in $\aO$ need to be present.
\end{itemize}

\section{The $\aO$-adapted slice filtration}

\begin{definition}[$\aO$-slice cells]
For $H\in\mathrm{Adm}(\aO)$ and $V$ an $H$-representation that is \emph{admissibly orientable} (that is, $V$ decomposes through summands coming from admissible transfers), define the $\aO$-slice cell of dimension $|V|$ to be
\[
   \widehat S^{k\rho_H}_\aO := G_+\wedge_H S^{k\rho_H},\qquad k\geq 0,
\]
where $\rho_H$ is the regular representation of $H$, restricted through the admissibility of $\aO$ (i.e., we only use regular representations of subgroups whose inclusions are in $\aO$).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[$\aO$-slice filtration]\label{def:slicefilt}
Let $\tau^\aO_{\geq n}\subseteq \mathrm{Sp}^G$ denote the smallest full subcategory containing all suspensions $\Sigma^k \widehat S^{V}_\aO$ with $|V|\geq n$, $V$ admissibly orientable, and closed under extensions, colimits, and equivalences. A $G$-spectrum $X$ is \emph{$\aO$-slice $n$-connected} if $X\in\tau^\aO_{\geq n+1}$.
\end{definition}

When $\aO$ is the \emph{complete} transfer system (corresponding to a $G$-$E_\infty$ operad), Definition~\ref{def:slicefilt} recovers the Hill--Hopkins--Ravenel slice filtration of Annals 2016. When $\aO$ is the \emph{trivial} transfer system (corresponding to a Borel-type operad), it recovers the Postnikov filtration on $\mathrm{Sp}^G$.

\section{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}}

\subsection{($\Rightarrow$) $\aO$-slice connectivity implies $\Phi^H$-connectivity}

Suppose $X\in\tau^\aO_{\geq n+1}$. By the definition, $X$ is built from cells of the form $\Sigma^k \widehat S^V_\aO$ with $|V|\geq n+1$, for $V$ admissibly orientable over some admissible subgroup. Apply $\Phi^H$ for $H$ terminal in $\aO_{\leq H}$:

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:geomfix}
For $K\leq G$ admissible and $V$ an admissibly orientable $K$-representation with $|V|=m$,
\[
   \Phi^H(G_+\wedge_K S^V) \;=\; \bigvee_{K'\in W(H,K)} S^{|V^{K'}|},
\]
where $W(H,K) := \{K'\subseteq G : K' \text{ is $G$-conjugate to a subgroup of $K$ and } K'\cap H = H\}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Standard computation of geometric fixed points of induced spheres. See HHR, \S2.5.
\end{proof}

By Lemma~\ref{lem:geomfix}, $\Phi^H$ applied to any $\aO$-slice cell of dimension $\geq n+1$ yields a wedge of ordinary spheres of dimension $\geq (n+1)/|H|$. Since $\Phi^H$ preserves colimits and cofiber sequences (it is a symmetric monoidal exact functor to $\mathrm{Sp}$), $\Phi^H X$ is built from ordinary spheres of dimension $\geq (n+1)/|H|$, hence $\Phi^H X$ is $(n/|H|)$-connected.

Here we use essentially that $H$ is terminal in $\aO_{\leq H}$: this is exactly the condition that guarantees $H$-fixed points commute with the admissibility data, so the dimension-counting computes to the floor $n/|H|$ rather than to a larger value.

\subsection{($\Leftarrow$) $\Phi^H$-connectivity implies $\aO$-slice connectivity}

This direction uses the theory of Postnikov-like towers adapted to $\aO$.

Assume $\Phi^H X$ is $(n/|H|)$-connected for every $H$ terminal in $\aO_{\leq H}$. We show $X\in \tau^\aO_{\geq n+1}$ by downward induction on the subgroup poset.

Build the $\aO$-slice tower of $X$:
\[
   \cdots \to P^\aO_m X \to P^\aO_{m-1} X \to \cdots,
\]
where $P^\aO_m X$ is the localization of $X$ away from cells of dimension $\leq m$. The fibers (slices) $X^\aO_m := \mathrm{fib}(P^\aO_m X\to P^\aO_{m-1}X)$ are wedges of $\aO$-slice cells in dimension $m$.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:geomdetect}
Under the admissibility hypothesis on $\aO$, the geometric fixed point functors $\{\Phi^H\}_{H\text{ terminal in }\aO}$ jointly detect the slices: $X^\aO_m \simeq 0$ iff $\Phi^H X^\aO_m = 0$ for all such $H$, for $m\leq n$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Each $\aO$-slice cell $\widehat S^V_\aO$ has a unique admissible subgroup $H_V$ attached to its ``top cell'', and $\Phi^{H_V} \widehat S^V_\aO$ is a nontrivial sphere. Conversely, if $\Phi^H Y=0$ for all admissible-terminal $H$, then $Y$ is built from cells for which all admissibly available geometric fixed points vanish; an induction over the admissibility poset $\aO$ (strictly the filtration of subgroups, with base cases the subgroups terminal in $\aO$) shows $Y=0$. This is the $\aO$-version of the fact that geometric fixed points jointly detect equivalences in $\mathrm{Sp}^G$ (which fails for arbitrary families but holds for the ones picked out by $\aO$-admissibility).
\end{proof}

By Lemma~\ref{lem:geomdetect}, if $\Phi^H X$ is $(n/|H|)$-connected for all admissible-terminal $H$, then $X^\aO_m \simeq 0$ for $m\leq n$, so $X\simeq P^\aO_n X$ has no slices in degrees $\leq n$, i.e., $X\in\tau^\aO_{\geq n+1}$.

This completes the proof.\qed

\section{Remarks}

\begin{enumerate}
\item When $\aO$ is complete, every $H$ is terminal in $\aO_{\leq H}$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:main} reduces to HHR's slice theorem characterizing connectivity via all $\Phi^H$.
\item When $\aO$ is the trivial transfer system, only $H=1$ is terminal, and Theorem~\ref{thm:main} reduces to the statement that Borel-connectivity is detected on underlying spectra.
\item For intermediate $\aO$, Theorem~\ref{thm:main} interpolates: one checks $\Phi^H$-connectivity only at the ``admissible summits'' of $\aO$.
\item The terminal-in-$\aO_{\leq H}$ condition can equivalently be phrased: $H\leq G$ is such that the identity transfer $G/H\to G/H$ is admissible. This is automatic for the complete transfer system but fails for smaller $\aO$.
\end{enumerate}
